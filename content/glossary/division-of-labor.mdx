---
term: Division of labor
slug: division-of-labor
short: Splitting work into small specialized tasks so everyone produces more.
related: [comparative-advantage, free-trade, market-economy]
---

Think about everything that happened before your phone reached your pocket: someone mined the metals, someone designed the chips, someone wrote the software, someone drove the delivery van. No single person on Earth could build that phone alone — and that's precisely the point.

This is the division of labor: splitting work into specialized tasks so that each person gets very good at one thing. Adam Smith opened *The Wealth of Nations* with a pin factory. One worker alone might make twenty pins a day; ten workers, each handling one step, made tens of thousands. Same people, wildly more pins.

Why does it work? Practice makes you faster, you stop losing time switching tasks, and specialists spot better tools and tricks. Smith added a famous catch: the division of labor is limited by the size of the market. A village supports a baker; only a city supports a wedding-cake specialist. It's one big reason trade makes everyone richer.
