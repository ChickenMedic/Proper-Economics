---
term: Rent-seeking
slug: rent-seeking
short: Chasing wealth through special favors and rules instead of creating value.
related: [incentive, tariff]
---

There are two ways to get rich off taxis. One: run better taxis - cleaner cars, faster pickups, lower fares. Two: persuade the city to cap the number of taxi licenses, then own the licenses. The second path made some license owners wealthy without a single passenger getting a better ride - in New York, a taxi medallion once traded for around a million dollars.

That second path is rent-seeking: spending effort to capture wealth through special privileges - licenses, tariffs, subsidies, favorable regulations - rather than by creating anything new. The "rent" is economist-speak for income above what competition would allow, and it has nothing to do with your landlord. Lobbying is its natural habitat.

The term comes from economist Anne Krueger, building on Gordon Tullock's work, and it's central to public choice economics - James Buchanan's project of studying politicians and lobbyists with the same cold eye economists turn on everyone else. The deep cost isn't just the unfair wins; it's the talent and money diverted into fighting over the pie instead of baking a bigger one.
