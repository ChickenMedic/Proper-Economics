---
term: Equilibrium
slug: equilibrium
short: The price where the amount buyers want equals the amount sellers offer.
related: [supply, demand, price-signal]
---

Think of a farmers' market near closing time. A seller with too many tomatoes left starts knocking the price down until they move. Another who sold out by ten in the morning makes a note: charge more next week. Week after week, the price drifts toward the level where the tomatoes on the table roughly match the shoppers who want them.

That resting point is equilibrium: the price at which the amount buyers want to buy equals the amount sellers want to sell. Price it higher and unsold goods pile up, pushing the price down. Price it lower and shelves empty out, pulling the price up. The market keeps nudging itself back toward the crossing point.

The startling part is that nobody sets this price. No committee, no decree - it finds itself, through millions of small adjustments. That quiet trick is much of what economists mean when they talk about markets "working."
