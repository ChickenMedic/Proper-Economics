---
term: Price signal
slug: price-signal
short: A price change that quietly tells everyone to buy less or make more.
related: [invisible-hand, central-planning, market-economy]
---

A frost wipes out half the coffee harvest in Brazil. You never hear about it - but a few months later your bag of beans costs more, so you brew slightly weaker pots, and somewhere a farmer in Vietnam decides this is the year to plant more coffee. You economized and she expanded, and neither of you needed to know about the frost.

That's a price signal. A price isn't just a number on a tag; it's a message. When something gets scarcer, its price rises, telling buyers "go easy" and sellers "we need more of this" - all without anyone writing a memo.

Friedrich Hayek made this the heart of his case for markets: knowledge about crops, tastes, and shortages is scattered across millions of people, and prices are how that knowledge travels. It's also why centrally planned economies struggled - freeze the prices, and the messages stop arriving.
