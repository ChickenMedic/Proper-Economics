---
term: Progressive tax
slug: progressive-tax
short: A tax that takes a bigger percentage from higher incomes.
related: [flat-tax, vat]
---

Suppose your neighbor earns five times what you do. Under a progressive income tax, she doesn't just pay five times the tax - she pays a *higher percentage* of her income than you do. The more you earn, the bigger the slice the tax collector takes. That's what "progressive" means here: the rate progresses upward with income. No political compliment intended.

The usual machinery is tax brackets, and they're widely misunderstood. Moving into "the 40% bracket" doesn't mean 40% of *everything* you earn - only the earnings above that bracket's threshold get taxed at 40%; everything below is taxed at the lower rates. Nobody ends up poorer by earning more, despite what your uncle insists at Thanksgiving.

The case for: a dollar means less to a millionaire than to a struggling family (diminishing marginal utility, applied to money), so those with more can bear more - a thought that goes back at least to Adam Smith. The case against: high top rates can blunt the incentive to work, invest, and stay put, an argument that fueled the tax-cutting turn of the 1980s. Nearly every rich country runs a progressive income tax; the fight is over how steep.
