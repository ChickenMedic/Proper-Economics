---
term: Elasticity
slug: elasticity
short: How strongly buyers or sellers react when the price changes.
related: [supply, demand]
---

When gas prices jump, you grumble — and then you fill the tank anyway, because you still have to get to work. But when your streaming service raises its price, canceling takes about thirty seconds. Same-sized price hike, wildly different reactions.

Elasticity is the measure of that difference: how much buyers (or sellers) change their behavior when the price moves. Gasoline is *inelastic* — the price changes, the behavior barely does, at least in the short run. Streaming subscriptions are *elastic* — a small price change sends customers scattering, because substitutes are a click away.

Alfred Marshall gave the idea its name, and it quietly runs much of the world. It's why addictive goods get taxed (smokers keep buying), why sales work on some products and not others, and why a bumper harvest can leave farmers *poorer* — everyone's crop gets bigger, but food demand barely budges, so prices collapse.
