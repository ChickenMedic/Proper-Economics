---
term: Supply
slug: supply
short: How much of something sellers are willing to offer at each price.
related: [demand, equilibrium, elasticity]
---

Imagine strawberries hit $10 a basket. Suddenly your neighbor with three bushes in her backyard is out there picking at dawn, and farms that skipped strawberries last year are planting them again. Nobody ordered anyone to grow more berries — the higher price did the persuading.

That's supply: the amount of a good that sellers are willing and able to offer at each possible price. As a rule, higher prices coax more of it out. A better price makes it worth working the extra hour, opening the second shop, or planting the fourth field.

Supply is one half of the most famous pair in economics. The other half — demand — pulls in the opposite direction, and where the two meet, a price settles. Alfred Marshall compared them to the two blades of a pair of scissors: ask which one does the cutting and you've missed the point.
