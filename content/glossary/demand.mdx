---
term: Demand
slug: demand
short: How much of something buyers want at each price.
related: [supply, equilibrium, elasticity]
---

Picture your favorite coffee shop raising the price of a latte to $9. The die-hards still show up, but you — and half the morning line — quietly switch to making coffee at home. Nothing about the coffee changed. The price did, and the crowd thinned.

That's demand: how much of something buyers are willing to purchase at each possible price. Almost always, a lower price means people buy more of it, and a higher price means they buy less. Economists draw this as a downward-sloping line, but you don't need the graph — you've lived it at the gas pump.

The key word is *willing*. Demand isn't how much people wish they had (infinite, presumably). It's what they'll actually pay for at a given price. Pair demand with supply and you get the machinery that sets prices in a market.
