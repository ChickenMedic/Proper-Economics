---
term: Invisible hand
slug: invisible-hand
short: How self-interested buying and selling can end up serving everyone.
related: [price-signal, laissez-faire, market-economy]
---

Your baker didn't get up at four in the morning because she loves you. She did it to earn a living. Yet there's the bread: fresh, affordable, and exactly where you wanted it. Adam Smith's famous line makes the point - we get our dinner not from the butcher's benevolence, but from his regard for his own interest.

The invisible hand is Smith's name for this quiet coordination. In a market with competition, people chasing their own goals end up serving others, because serving others is how you get paid. Nobody plans the outcome; it emerges, as if guided by - well, you get the metaphor.

One caution: Smith used the phrase sparingly and never claimed markets fix everything. Pollution, monopolies, and things markets undersupply are real gaps, and later economists spent careers mapping them. The invisible hand is a tendency worth marveling at, not a guarantee.
